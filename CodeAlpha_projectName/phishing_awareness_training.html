What Is Phishing?
Phishing is a social engineering attack where criminals impersonate trusted entities — banks, employers, tech companies — to trick victims into revealing passwords, financial data, or installing malware.

Common phishing types:

📧
Email PhishingMass emails pretending to be trusted brands like PayPal, Microsoft, or your bank.
🎯
Spear PhishingTargeted attacks using personal info about you — name, employer, colleagues.
📱
Smishing (SMS)Fake text messages with malicious links — "Your package is stuck, click here."
📞
Vishing (Voice)Phone calls from fake tech support, IRS, or bank fraud departments.
👑
WhalingPhishing aimed at executives (CEO, CFO) for large financial transfers.
🕳️
Clone PhishingA real email you received is cloned with malicious links swapped in.
⚠️ The Goal: Attackers want your credentials, personal data, or to install malware. They exploit urgency, fear, and trust — not technical vulnerabilities.

Spotting Phishing Emails

Hover over the highlighted parts of this real-world phishing email below to reveal why they're suspicious.

From: security-alert@paypa1-support.com🚩 Domain is "paypa1" (number 1 instead of letter L). Not the real PayPal domain!
To: you@yourcompany.com
Subject: ⚠️ URGENT: Your account will be SUSPENDED in 24 hours!🚩 Manufactured urgency is a classic phishing tactic to make you act without thinking.

Dear Valued Customer🚩 Generic greeting. Legitimate companies use your actual name. They don't know who you are!,

We have detected suspicious login activity on your PayPal account. To avoid suspension, you must verify your identity immediately🚩 Urgency language pressures you to skip careful thinking..

Please click the secure link below to confirm your account details🚩 PayPal will NEVER ask you to "confirm details" via an unsolicited email link.:

http://paypal-secure-verify.suspicious-domain.ru/login🚩 Real URL is NOT paypal.com — it's a completely different domain. The word "paypal" in the path doesn't make it safe!

Failure to verify within 24 hours🚩 Artificial deadline. Designed to create panic and bypass your logical thinking. will result in permanent account restriction.

Thank you,
The PayPal Security Team🚩 Anyone can sign an email "PayPal Security Team." Check the actual sending domain!

LESSON 3 OF 4
Social Engineering Tactics
Attackers exploit human psychology, not just technology. Understanding how they manipulate you is the best defense.
😨 Fear & Urgency

"Your account has been hacked!" / "Pay now or face legal action!" — Fear bypasses rational thinking. Attackers want you to panic and click before you verify.

🛡️ Defense: Stop. Breathe. Verify independently. Call the company on a number YOU look up, not one in the email.
🤵 Authority & Impersonation

"This is the IRS." / "Email from CEO: send $10,000 in gift cards immediately." People comply with authority figures without questioning.

🛡️ Defense: Always verify unusual requests via a second channel. Your CEO can wait 5 minutes for a phone callback.
🎁 Curiosity & Greed

"You've won a $500 Amazon gift card!" / "See who viewed your profile." Attractive offers or intriguing mysteries override skepticism.

🛡️ Defense: If it sounds too good to be true, it is. Legitimate prizes don't require clicking unsolicited links.
🤝 Trust & Familiarity

"Hi, this is John from IT — we need your password to fix an issue." / Emails appearing to come from colleagues. We lower our guard with familiar faces and names.

🛡️ Defense: IT will never ask for your password. No legitimate service will either — ever.
LESSON 4 OF 4
Best Practices & Prevention
✅ The SLAM Method — a quick mental checklist for any suspicious message.
📤
S
Sender
Is the sender's email address legitimate?
🔗
L
Links
Hover over links — do they go where expected?
📎
A
Attachments
Were you expecting this file? Is the type suspicious?
💬
M
Message
Does the message make sense? Grammar errors? Urgency?

Essential security habits:

🔐Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all accounts. Even if attackers steal your password, MFA blocks access.
🔑Use a password manager with unique passwords per site. Password reuse means one breach = all accounts compromised.
🔄Keep software and OS up to date. Many phishing attacks exploit unpatched vulnerabilities.
🚨Report suspicious emails to your IT/security team. Don't delete them — reporting helps protect everyone.
❌Never enter credentials after clicking an email link. Go directly to the website by typing the URL yourself.
